I would be very careful here.

A lot of people accidentally turn:

> "I want enough LeetCode for interviews and programming strength"

into

> "I spent 18 months grinding 700 LeetCode problems."

For your roadmap, that's a terrible trade.

You are already building:

- games
- simulations
- backend systems
- AI systems

Those are where most of your growth will come from.

LeetCode should be a **supplement**, not a career.

My target would be roughly:

```text
50 - 80 carefully selected problems
```

and then stop.

# Where I Would Stop

I would NOT continue into:

- advanced DP
- segment trees
- Fenwick trees
- union find
- advanced graph theory
- competitive programming
- hard LeetCode obsession

For your roadmap:

```text
Python
↓
OOP Snake
↓
Functional Snake
↓
DSA
↓
50-80 LeetCode-style problems
↓
10 Games/Simulations
↓
Backend
↓
AI
```

is a much stronger path than:

```text
Python
↓
700 LeetCode problems
↓
nothing else
```

The first path creates an engineer. The second mostly creates a candidate who is good at coding interviews.
